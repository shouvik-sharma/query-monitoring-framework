## Personal Statement

I believe data engineering's future lies at the intersection of AI and infrastructure. Most data teams spend too much time on manual monitoring and debugging—tasks that machines can do better and faster. My mission is to build tools that automate the tedious parts of data ops, freeing engineers to focus on high-impact work.

This project started as a hackweek idea—a way to explore whether LLMs could understand SQL semantics in production. After 3 months of real-world testing, I'm convinced they can. More importantly, I'm convinced that the data engineering community is ready to adopt AI-powered tooling, not just as science experiments, but as production-grade systems.

I'm publishing this research to show what's possible and to invite others to build on these ideas. The code is open source. The lessons learned are documented. The door is open for collaborators. Let's build the future of data operations together.
